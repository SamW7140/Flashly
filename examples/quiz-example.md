## When did the Roman Empire fall?
??
The Western Roman Empire fell in 476 CE when the last emperor, Romulus Augustulus, was deposed by the Germanic chieftain Odoacer.

The Eastern Roman Empire (Byzantine Empire) continued until 1453 CE.

## What was the significance of the Magna Carta?
??
The Magna Carta (1215) was a landmark document that established the principle that everyone, including the king, is subject to the law.

Key principles:
- Limited royal authority
- Protection of legal rights
- Foundation for constitutional law
- Influenced modern democratic systems

## What is the Renaissance?
??
The Renaissance was a period of cultural rebirth in Europe from the 14th to 17th century.

**Characteristics:**
- Revival of classical learning
- Emphasis on humanism
- Advances in art and science
- Beginning of the modern age

**Notable figures:** Leonardo da Vinci, Michelangelo, Galileo

## What was the Cold War?
??
The Cold War (1947-1991) was a period of geopolitical tension between the United States and the Soviet Union and their respective allies.

**Key features:**
- Nuclear arms race
- Proxy wars
- Space race
- Ideological conflict (capitalism vs. communism)
- No direct military conflict between superpowers

## What caused World War I?
??
World War I (1914-1918) was caused by multiple factors:

1. **Militarism** - Arms race and military buildup
2. **Alliances** - Complex web of treaties
3. **Imperialism** - Colonial competition
4. **Nationalism** - Ethnic tensions and rivalries

**Immediate trigger:** Assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand in Sarajevo (June 28, 1914)

## What was the significance of the Battle of Hastings?
??
The Battle of Hastings (1066) was fought between the Norman-French army of William the Conqueror and the English army under King Harold II.

**Significance:**
- Norman conquest of England
- End of Anglo-Saxon rule
- Introduction of French culture and language
- Changed English social structure
- Led to the Domesday Book survey

**Result:** William became King of England

## What is democracy?
??
Democracy is a system of government where power is vested in the people, who rule either directly or through freely elected representatives.

**Types:**
- **Direct democracy** - Citizens vote on laws directly
- **Representative democracy** - Citizens elect representatives

**Key principles:**
- Free and fair elections
- Rule of law
- Protection of individual rights
- Majority rule with minority rights

**Origin:** Ancient Greece (Athens, 5th century BCE)

## Quiz Generation Notes

This deck is ideal for quiz generation because:

1. **Multiple Choice Potential:**
   - Years (1945, 1776, 1989, 1789) make good distractors
   - Historical figures (Washington, Gagarin, Gutenberg)
   - Places (Paris, Egypt, France)

2. **Fill-in-the-Blank:**
   - Multiple cloze deletions provide natural fill-blank questions
   - Clear single-word or short phrase answers

3. **True/False:**
   - Factual statements can be converted to T/F
   - Dates and names are verifiable

4. **AI Enhancement:**
   - Rich context for creative question generation
   - Can create inference questions
   - Can generate "what-if" scenarios
   - Can test deeper understanding

**Expected Cards:** ~50+ (including multiple cloze deletions)

**Quiz Recommendation:**
- Traditional: 20 questions mixed types
- AI: 15 questions with explanations and context
